Dashcam Upload Instructions

To provide step-by-step instructions on how to set up the phone camera for data collection. By following the instructions, you will be able to position and configure their camera in a way that allows you to capture the necessary data. This will cover the necessary steps for setting up the camera, including positioning, settings, and ensuring that the images captured meet the requirements for valid data collection. You can expect to gain an understanding of how to set up the camera effectively for data collection and ensure that the videos are suitable for upload and processing.

Camera Setup

  1. Enable the location feature on your phone before recording videos of the road. This will ensure that there is metadata with geolocation information that can be extracted by the system.
  2. Mount the camera on top of the dashboard or near the flat mirror, positioning it in the middle to capture the road scenes effectively.
  3. Before starting your drive, it's important to begin recording using the third-party application. To ensure that the footage is synchronized, make sure that both the third-party application and your phone's built-in camera are recording simultaneously.
  4. After you have finished recording, it's important to check the file to ensure that it is a valid recording. This can be done by reviewing the file and making sure that it contains the entire duration of your drive and that the footage is clear and unobstructed. You can also check if the file is compatible with the third-party application that you are using. If the file is not valid, you may need to re-record your drive to ensure that you have a reliable and accurate record of your trip."

Third-party application

  • Download the GPS Logger app or any similar app on your phone and open the application.
  • Take Note: You may use any third-party application similar to GPS Logges as long as it can get the latitude, longitude and altitude of the data.
  • For GPS Logger
    1. Open the app and click the "Record" button to start the GPS tracking.
    2. Once the GPS Logger starts recording, you may open your phone camera app and start recording video.
    3. After recording, stop the GPS Logger app to end the tracking session. The GPS data will be saved in the track list.
    4. To access the recorded data, click on any of the tracks in the list.
    5. On the top right corner of the screen, you will find options to edit, share, download, and delete the track.
    6. Download the track to generate a GPX file, which will contain information such as latitude, longitude, altitude, time, and speed.
